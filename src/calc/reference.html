Tricks for Quotients (Using Natural Logarithm Properties)

Preliminary
\begin{equation} \begin{split} \ln(ab) &= \ln(a) \cdot \ln(b) \\ \ln\left(\frac{a}{b}\right) &= \ln(a) - \ln(b) \\ \ln(a^r) &= r \cdot \ln(a) \\ \end{split} \end{equation} \newcommand{\derivative}{\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\;} \begin{equation} \begin{split} \derivative \ln(x) &= \frac{1}{x} \\ \derivative \ln(x) &= \frac{\derivative x}{x} \\ \end{split} \end{equation}
Example
\begin{align*} \newcommand{\derivative}{\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\;} \overbrace{y = \frac{(x + 1)^5}{(x - 3)^{\frac{1}{2}}}}^{\text{multiply by $\ln$}} \\\\ \overbrace{ \begin{align*} \ln(y) &= \ln \left( \frac{(x + 1)^5}{(x - 3)^{\frac{1}{2}}} \right) \\ \\ \ln(y) &= \ln \left((x + 1)^5\right) - \ln\left((x - 3)^{\frac{1}{2}}\right) \\ \\ \ln(y) &= 5\ln(x + 1) - \frac{1}{2}\ln(x - 3) \end{align*} }^{\text{apply log properties}} \\\\ \overbrace{ \begin{align*} \frac{y^{\prime}}{y} &= 5 \frac{1}{x + 1} - \frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{x - 3} \\ \\ \frac{y^{\prime}}{y} &= \frac{5}{x + 1} - \frac{1}{2x - 6} \\ \end{align*} }^{\text{simplification}} \\\\ \overbrace{y^{\prime} = \left(\frac{5}{x + 1} - \frac{1}{2x - 6}\right) \cdot y}^{\text{multiplied by $y$}} \\\\ \overbrace{y^{\prime} = \left(\frac{5}{x + 1} - \frac{1}{2x - 6}\right) \cdot \frac{(x + 1)^5}{(x - 3)^{\frac{1}{2}}}}^{\text{substituted the expression for $y$}} \end{align*} Therefore: \begin{equation} \begin{split} f(x) &= \frac{(x + 1)^5}{(x - 3)^{\frac{1}{2}}} \\ f^\prime(x) &= \left(\frac{5}{x + 1} - \frac{1}{2x - 6}\right) \cdot \frac{(x + 1)^5}{(x - 3)^{\frac{1}{2}}} \\ \end{split} \end{equation}
